# World-Class Content Scoring Rubric

**Minimum to Publish**: 9.3/10
**Target**: 9.5+/10

---

## How to Score

After completing all seven editing passes, score each dimension below on a 0-10 scale. Calculate weighted average. Round to one decimal place.

**Scoring Discipline**:
- Be HARSH. World-class is rare for a reason.
- 10/10 is "best in the world" - almost never achieved
- 9/10 is "exceptional" - this should be uncommon
- 8/10 is "very good" - solid work but not world-class
- 7/10 is "good" - acceptable but not publishable here
- <7/10 is unacceptable - major revision or reject

---

## Dimension 1: Philosophical Accuracy (Weight: 15%)

**Evaluating**: Correct application of Deutsch's framework, no distortions

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Deutsch himself would approve every detail. Framework applied with perfect precision. |
| 9 | Minor imprecision in one place, but overall excellent application. |
| 8 | Good understanding, but some concepts could be sharper. |
| 7 | Mostly correct but has simplifications that lose meaning. |
| 6 | Several inaccuracies or misapplications. |
| <6 | Fundamental misunderstanding of framework. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

---

## Dimension 2: Factual Accuracy (Weight: 10%)

**Evaluating**: All facts, statistics, quotes, examples are correct and sourced

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Every fact verified, all sources impeccable, zero errors. |
| 9 | One very minor detail uncited but clearly true. |
| 8 | All major facts correct, minor citation gaps. |
| 7 | One or two factual issues that could be questioned. |
| 6 | Multiple factual concerns. |
| <6 | Factual errors that undermine credibility. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

---

## Dimension 3: Logical Soundness (Weight: 15%)

**Evaluating**: Arguments valid, no fallacies, counterarguments addressed

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Airtight reasoning, strongest counterarguments steelmanned and addressed, no logical gaps. |
| 9 | Very strong logic, one counterargument could be slightly stronger. |
| 8 | Sound reasoning but one argument could be tightened. |
| 7 | Generally valid but has a logical gap or weak counterargument treatment. |
| 6 | Multiple logical issues or strawmanned objections. |
| <6 | Fallacies present or major logical flaws. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

---

## Dimension 4: Clarity & Accessibility (Weight: 15%)

**Evaluating**: Target audience can easily understand, no unnecessary complexity

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Crystal clear to target audience, no sentence requires re-reading, flows effortlessly. |
| 9 | Very clear, one or two sentences could be slightly simpler. |
| 8 | Clear overall but 2-3 passages require effort to parse. |
| 7 | Mostly clear but several unclear sections. |
| 6 | Frequently unclear or confusing. |
| <6 | Target audience would struggle to understand. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

---

## Dimension 5: Engagement & Writing Quality (Weight: 15%)

**Evaluating**: Hook, flow, examples, prose quality, momentum, authentic voice

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Impossible to stop reading, every paragraph earns its place, prose is elegant yet feels human—uses contractions naturally, varies sentence rhythm dramatically, includes personality through asides and tangents that connect back, acknowledges the reader's likely thoughts, sounds like a smart friend explaining something fascinating. |
| 9 | Highly engaging, authentic voice throughout with natural rhythm and personality, one or two sections could be slightly more compelling or have minor stiffness. |
| 8 | Good engagement but has 1-2 slower sections, OR authentic voice inconsistent (some sections feel robotic while others feel human). |
| 7 | Holds attention but not compelling throughout, OR prose feels formal/processed rather than authentically human. |
| 6 | Loses reader interest in multiple places, OR reads like a document rather than a person writing. |
| <6 | Boring, robotic, or hard to stay engaged with. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

**Authentic Voice Sub-Checks** (required for 9+ score):
- Contractions: Natural use throughout? (not forced formality)
- Rhythm: Mix of short punches and longer flows? (not monotonous)
- Personality: Asides, tangents, second thoughts visible? (not anonymous prose)
- Reader awareness: Acknowledges reader's likely reactions? (not lecturing into void)

**CRITICAL**: Authenticity never compromises clarity. If an informal phrase is unclear, it fails—choose clarity over casualness every time.

---

## Dimension 6: Practical Utility (Weight: 20%) - CRITICAL

**Evaluating**: Specific, actionable steps; concrete examples; immediate applicability

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | 5+ specific, detailed examples. Step-by-step workflows. Reader can act in next 24hrs. Templates/frameworks provided. |
| 9 | 3-4 strong examples. Clear steps. Immediately actionable. Minor details could be more specific. |
| 8 | 2-3 examples. Generally actionable but some vagueness. |
| 7 | 1-2 examples. More conceptual than practical. |
| 6 | Vague advice like "think about" or "consider" without specifics. |
| <6 | No practical application or purely theoretical. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

**Sub-scores to check**:
- Specificity of examples: __/10 (minimum 8)
- Immediacy (can do in 24hrs): __/10 (minimum 7)
- Completeness of steps: __/10 (minimum 8)

---

## Dimension 7: Intellectual Honesty (Weight: 10%)

**Evaluating**: Limitations acknowledged, uncertainty expressed, counterarguments substantial

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Perfectly honest about limits, uncertainty where appropriate, 3+ strong counterarguments steelmanned. |
| 9 | Very honest, 2-3 counterarguments well addressed, minor hedge missing somewhere. |
| 8 | Honest overall but one area overclaims or one counterargument underwhelming. |
| 7 | Generally honest but misses important limitation or counterargument. |
| 6 | Overclaims in multiple places or strawmans objections. |
| <6 | False certainty or dishonest representation. |

**Minimum acceptable**: 9/10

---

## Final Calculation

```
Score = (Philosophical × 0.15) + (Factual × 0.10) + (Logical × 0.15) +
        (Clarity × 0.15) + (Engagement × 0.15) + (Practical × 0.20) +
        (Honesty × 0.10)
```

**Example**:
```
Philosophical: 9.5 × 0.15 = 1.425
Factual: 9.0 × 0.10 = 0.900
Logical: 9.5 × 0.15 = 1.425
Clarity: 9.0 × 0.15 = 1.350
Engagement: 9.0 × 0.15 = 1.350
Practical: 9.5 × 0.20 = 1.900
Honesty: 9.5 × 0.10 = 0.950
────────────────────────
Total: 9.3/10 ✓ (barely passes)
```

## What Each Score Band Feels Like

**9.5-10.0**: "This is as good as The New Yorker / Wait But Why / Paul Graham essays / Best of David Deutsch"

**9.3-9.4**: "This is excellent. I'd happily share this with anyone. Minor tweaks would make it exceptional."

**9.0-9.2**: "This is very good but not quite world-class. Needs polish in 1-2 areas."

**8.5-8.9**: "This is good work but doesn't stand out. Multiple areas need improvement."

**8.0-8.4**: "Decent but not publication-ready. Needs substantial work."

**<8.0**: "Not ready. Fundamental issues present."

---

## Common Reasons Content Fails to Reach 9.3

1. **Weak practical examples** (kills Practical Utility score)
   - "Consider your goals" instead of "Write down your last 3 goals. For each, ask..."

2. **Insufficient counterarguments** (kills Logical Soundness & Honesty scores)
   - Only 1 objection addressed, or objections strawmanned

3. **Preachy tone** (kills Engagement score)
   - "You must understand..." instead of "Consider..."

4. **Unclear passages** (kills Clarity score)
   - Requires re-reading, assumptions unstated

5. **Weak examples** (kills Engagement & Practical scores)
   - Abstract instead of concrete, generic instead of specific

6. **No immediate action** (kills Practical Utility score)
   - Philosophy without "what to do in next 5 minutes"

7. **Robotic, formal voice** (kills Engagement score)
   - All sentences same length, no contractions, no personality
   - Sounds like document, not person

## Calibration Examples

### 10/10 in Practical Utility

```
Right now, open a document and try this:

1. Write down a goal you currently have
2. Write your explanation for why this goal matters
3. Pick any word in that explanation and change it to a synonym
4. If the explanation still "makes sense," it's easy-to-vary (bad)

Example:
- Original: "I want to lose weight to feel healthier"
- Test: "I want to lose weight to feel happier"
- Still makes sense? → Easy to vary → Bad explanation

A good explanation:
"I want to lose 15 pounds because my blood pressure is 145/95 and my doctor says
losing weight will likely bring it below 130/85, reducing my stroke risk."

Try changing any detail - the specificity makes it hard to vary.

Do this exercise now for one of your goals. It takes 3 minutes.
```

### 6/10 in Practical Utility (UNACCEPTABLE)

```
To apply this framework to your goals, think carefully about what you're trying to
achieve. Consider whether your goals are well-formulated. Reflect on the difference
between good and bad explanations in the context of your personal situation. Be
thoughtful about how you approach goal-setting going forward.
```

**Why it fails**: No specific steps, no concrete examples, full of "think about" and "consider", can't actually do anything with this.

### 10/10 in Authentic Voice (Component of Engagement)

```
Here's the thing about goals—and I realize this might sound backwards at first—the best ones don't actually feel like "goals" at all.

Let me explain. (And yes, I know that sounds like a cop-out. Bear with me.)

Most goal-setting advice tells you to be specific. "Lose weight" becomes "Lose 15 pounds by March." Fine. That's better. But it's still missing something crucial.

I once spent six months chasing a goal that looked perfect on paper. Specific? Check. Measurable? Absolutely. Time-bound? You bet. And when I finally hit it, I felt... nothing. Actually, that's not true—I felt confused. Where was the satisfaction?

The problem wasn't the goal. It was the explanation behind it.

Think about it this way: you can swap "15 pounds" for "20 pounds" and the goal still makes sense. You can swap "March" for "June" and nothing really changes. That flexibility? That's a red flag.
```

### 6/10 in Authentic Voice (UNACCEPTABLE)

```
Goal-setting is an important practice for achieving success. Many individuals fail to set proper goals, which leads to suboptimal outcomes. It is essential to understand the difference between effective and ineffective goal formulation. Research has demonstrated that specific goals produce better results than vague aspirations. Therefore, one should endeavor to create goals that are measurable and time-bound. This approach will yield superior results in most contexts.
```

**Why it fails**: No contractions, monotonous sentence length, no personality, no reader acknowledgment, sounds like a committee wrote it.
