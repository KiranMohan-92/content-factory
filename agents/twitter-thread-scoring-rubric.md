# Twitter Thread Scoring Rubric (Viral Optimization)

## Purpose
Evaluate Twitter threads for both intellectual rigor (Deutsch framework) AND algorithmic optimization (X engagement signals). A thread must score well on BOTH dimensions to be considered "banger" quality.

---

## Scoring Dimensions

### 1. Hook Power (25%)
**What it measures**: Will people stop scrolling and click into the thread?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Impossible to scroll past. Creates immediate FOMO. Specific, contrarian, emotionally resonant. Would go viral as standalone tweet. |
| 9 | Excellent hook. Strong curiosity gap. Clear pattern interrupt. Most people would click. |
| 8 | Good hook. Creates interest. Some specificity. Above average engagement expected. |
| 7 | Decent hook. Works but not remarkable. Could be stronger. |
| 6 | Weak hook. Too vague or generic. Easy to scroll past. |
| 5 | Poor hook. No curiosity gap. Sounds like everyone else. |
| <5 | Failed hook. Would actively turn people away. |

**Key Questions**:
- Would this stop YOUR scroll?
- Is there a specific number, name, or claim?
- Does it challenge common belief or create paradox?
- Is there FOMO if you don't read?

---

### 2. Dwell Time Optimization (20%)
**What it measures**: Will people read all the way through?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Impossible to stop reading. Perfect pacing. Strategic cliffhangers. Each tweet pulls to next. White space perfect. |
| 9 | Excellent flow. Clear cliffhangers. No dead spots. Easy mobile reading. |
| 8 | Good pacing. Some cliffhangers. Mostly engaging throughout. |
| 7 | Decent flow. Occasional slow spots. Could tighten. |
| 6 | Uneven pacing. Some skippable tweets. Dense in places. |
| 5 | Poor flow. Multiple dead spots. Walls of text. |
| <5 | Unreadable. Dense blocks. No cliffhangers. Reader drops off early. |

**Key Questions**:
- Are there cliffhangers at tweets 5-6 and 10-11?
- Is each tweet under 250 characters?
- Are there short lines with white space?
- Does every tweet advance or reveal something?

---

### 3. Intellectual Rigor (15%)
**What it measures**: Is the content substantively valuable (Deutsch framework)?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Profound insight. Clear mechanism. Hard-to-vary explanation. Changes how reader thinks. |
| 9 | Excellent insight. Well-explained mechanism. Strong evidence. |
| 8 | Good insight. Mechanism present. Mostly hard-to-vary. |
| 7 | Decent insight. Some mechanism. Could be more rigorous. |
| 6 | Surface-level insight. Vague mechanism. Easy-to-vary explanation. |
| 5 | Common knowledge restated. No mechanism. Platitudes. |
| <5 | Wrong or misleading. No intellectual value. |

**Key Questions**:
- Is there a clear mechanism (not just assertion)?
- Would the explanation break if you varied its components?
- Does it explain more than intended (reach)?
- Is the strongest objection addressed?

---

### 4. Quotability/Saveability (15%)
**What it measures**: Will people screenshot, save, or quote-tweet individual tweets?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Multiple tweets are standalone bangers. At least 3 highly screenshot-worthy. Numbered frameworks. Quotable one-liners. |
| 9 | Several quotable tweets. At least 2 would spread as standalone. |
| 8 | Good quotability. One or two strong standalone tweets. |
| 7 | Decent. A few tweets work alone but not remarkable. |
| 6 | Weak standalone value. Tweets only make sense in context. |
| 5 | No quotable content. Nothing worth saving. |
| <5 | Actively bad quotes. Would embarrass if shared. |

**Key Questions**:
- Which tweets would work as standalone posts?
- Is there a numbered framework people would screenshot?
- Are there visual patterns (arrows, equals, vs)?
- Would YOU save/bookmark this?

---

### 5. Engagement Design (15%)
**What it measures**: Is the thread engineered to generate algorithm-boosting engagement?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | CTA designed for maximum reply generation. Specific question invites responses. Save prompt explicit. Follow hook compelling. Author will clearly engage with replies. |
| 9 | Strong CTA. Multiple engagement vectors. Will generate replies. |
| 8 | Good CTA. Clear call to engage. Reply prompt present. |
| 7 | Decent CTA. Some engagement prompt. Could be stronger. |
| 6 | Weak CTA. Generic "thoughts?" No specific prompt. |
| 5 | Poor CTA. No reply prompt. Just ends. |
| <5 | No CTA. Thread just stops. |

**Key Questions**:
- Does CTA have save prompt, reply prompt, AND follow hook?
- Is the reply prompt specific enough to generate responses?
- Will author realistically engage with replies (75x boost)?
- Is there a challenge/action readers can take?

---

### 6. Specificity (10%)
**What it measures**: Are claims backed by concrete, memorable details?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Every claim has specific evidence. Names, numbers, dates. At least 4 concrete examples. Nothing vague. |
| 9 | Excellent specificity. 3+ strong examples. Numbers and names present. |
| 8 | Good specificity. 2-3 concrete examples. Some numbers. |
| 7 | Decent. Some specificity. 1-2 examples. |
| 6 | Vague. Generic examples. Few specifics. |
| 5 | Abstract throughout. No concrete details. |
| <5 | Unsupported claims. No evidence at all. |

**Key Questions**:
- Are there specific numbers (272 words, 90%, 5 characters)?
- Are there named examples (Lincoln, Einstein)?
- Are analogies concrete ("JPEG to pixels" not "encoding mismatch")?
- Could someone fact-check the examples?

---

## Scoring Calculation

```
Final Score = (Hook × 0.25) + (Dwell × 0.20) + (Rigor × 0.15) +
              (Quotability × 0.15) + (Engagement × 0.15) + (Specificity × 0.10)
```

### Thresholds

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 9.0+ | **BANGER** | Publish immediately. High viral potential. |
| 8.5-8.9 | **Strong** | Publish. Will perform well. |
| 8.0-8.4 | **Good** | Publish with minor tweaks. |
| 7.5-7.9 | **Acceptable** | Revise weak dimensions before publishing. |
| 7.0-7.4 | **Needs Work** | Significant revision required. |
| <7.0 | **Reject** | Rewrite from scratch. |

## Quick Evaluation Matrix

For rapid assessment, check these 10 boxes:

### Hook (must have 3/3)
- [ ] Specific (number, name, or claim)
- [ ] Contrarian or counterintuitive
- [ ] Creates curiosity gap (FOMO)

### Body (must have 5/6)
- [ ] Cliffhanger at tweet 5-6
- [ ] At least 3 specific examples
- [ ] Each tweet under 250 chars
- [ ] Mechanism clearly explained
- [ ] Strongest objection addressed
- [ ] At least 2 quotable/saveable tweets

### CTA (must have 2/3)
- [ ] Save/bookmark prompt
- [ ] Reply-generating question
- [ ] Follow hook with reason

**Minimum viable**: 10/12 boxes checked

## Comparison: Before/After Optimization

### Weak Thread Signals
- "Here's what I learned about X"
- Long paragraphs
- Generic examples
- Ends with "Thoughts?"
- No cliffhangers
- Abstract language

### Banger Thread Signals
- "[Specific claim] is wrong. Here's why:"
- Short lines, white space
- Names, numbers, dates
- Ends with specific question + save + follow
- Strategic cliffhangers every 2-3 tweets
- Concrete analogies

## Editor Instructions

When evaluating a Twitter thread:

1. **Score each dimension 1-10** using criteria above
2. **Calculate weighted average**
3. **Identify weakest dimension** for targeted improvement
4. **Check the 10-box matrix** for minimum viability
5. **Compare to banger signals** for qualitative assessment

**Output format**:
